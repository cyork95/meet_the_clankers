# Meet the Clankers - Publishing Schedule Recommendations

## TL;DR Recommendation
- **Introduction Episode**: Tuesday, 5:00 AM ET
- **Daily Episodes**: Weekdays (Mon-Fri), 5:00 AM ET
- **Your Lunchtime Idea**: Not optimal for daily news, but could work for special episodes

---

## Why Early Morning (5 AM ET) is Best

### Data-Driven Reasons:
1. **Peak Download Time**: 5 AM ET sees the highest download numbers across all podcasts
2. **Feed Positioning**: Episodes appear at the top of listeners' feeds before the morning rush
3. **Commute Timing**: Available for 8 AM commuters (your target audience)
4. **Competition**: Avoids the midnight-2 AM burial zone where episodes get lost

### Listener Behavior:
- **71%** of podcast listeners tune in during commutes
- **Peak listening times**: 8 AM and 5 PM (commuting hours)
- **Weekday preference**: Most consumption happens Monday-Friday
- **Multitasking**: People listen while commuting, doing chores, working out

### Successful Examples:
- **The Daily (NYT)**: Uploads by 6 AM ET on weekdays
- **The NewsWorthy**: Early morning release for commuters
- **Most daily news podcasts**: 2-6 AM ET release window

---

## Your Lunchtime Idea: Pros & Cons

### Why Lunchtime (12 PM ET) Could Work:
✅ **Lunch break listening**: People check phones during lunch  
✅ **Midday refresh**: Catches people who missed morning content  
✅ **Less competition**: Fewer podcasts release at noon  
✅ **International audience**: Better for European listeners (5 PM GMT)

### Why Lunchtime is Suboptimal:
❌ **Missed commuters**: Your primary audience already chose their morning podcast  
❌ **Feed burial**: Morning releases push you down the feed  
❌ **News freshness**: Daily news is expected in the morning  
❌ **Habit formation**: Harder to build a "morning routine" habit  
❌ **Lower overall downloads**: Data shows morning releases perform better

## Recommended Publishing Schedule

### Introduction Episode: "Who or What Is This?"
**Recommended:** Tuesday, 5:00 AM ET

**Why Tuesday?**
- Mid-week has highest engagement (not Monday chaos, not Friday wind-down)
- Gives you Monday to promote "New podcast dropping tomorrow!"
- Tuesdays-Thursdays see the most podcast downloads
- Builds anticipation for Wednesday's first daily episode

**Alternative:** Wednesday, 5:00 AM ET
- Start the week mid-stride
- First daily episode on Thursday
- Gives listeners time to discover before weekend

### Daily Episodes
**Recommended:** Monday-Friday, 5:00 AM ET

**Schedule:**
- **Monday**: Week kickoff, set the tone
- **Tuesday**: Peak engagement day
- **Wednesday**: Most popular publishing day
- **Thursday**: Highest total downloads
- **Friday**: Week wrap-up, lighter tone

**Weekends:** 
- Take a break (daily news podcasts typically skip weekends)
- OR publish a special "Weekend Roundup" at 8 AM Saturday

## Publishing Workflow

### For 5:00 AM ET Release:
1. **Generate episode**: Night before (8-10 PM)
2. **Review/edit**: Night before (10-11 PM)
3. **Schedule upload**: Set to publish at 5:00 AM ET
4. **Promote**: Tweet/post at 7:00 AM when people wake up

### For 12:00 PM ET Release:
1. **Generate episode**: Morning (8-9 AM)
2. **Review/edit**: Morning (9-11 AM)
3. **Upload**: 11:45 AM for 12:00 PM release
4. **Promote**: Post at 12:00 PM when episode drops

## Final Thoughts

**The Data Says:** 5:00 AM ET on weekdays is optimal for daily news podcasts.

**But Remember:** 
- **Consistency > Perfect Timing**: Better to publish at noon consistently than 5 AM sporadically
- **Sustainability Matters**: Choose a time you can maintain long-term
- **Audience Building**: Your audience will adapt to YOUR schedule if you're consistent
- **Quality > Timing**: A great episode at noon beats a mediocre episode at 5 AM

**My Suggestion:** 
Start with the introduction at 5:00 AM ET on Tuesday to maximize discovery. Then decide:
- If you can sustain 5 AM releases → stick with it for maximum growth
- If lunchtime is more sustainable → switch to noon and own that schedule

The most important thing is **consistency**. Pick a time and stick to it religiously.
